Analysing X users' sentiment on the Covid-19 Pandemic

The probability of your organisation's increased market share only increases if your brand itself becomes top of mind for current and prospective customers. A popular brand does not equal a great or desired brand, as populartity could mean putting on one of two hats (Negative/Positive). When this concept is properly understood, your goal then becomes to ensure that your brand popularity takes an upward trajectory while putting on the positive hat or at worst, maximizing the amount of times your brand shows up to the party wearing a positive hat.

How then do you know what hat your organisation's popularity currently rocks? You don't, except you measure. While we may not be able to outrightly measure the positivity or negativity of your brand's popularity, we can definitely measure quantitative attributes related to your brand, one of which is a measure of your brand's Positive to Negative sentiment ratio i.e., what are the great things being said about your brand versus the negative things being said about your brand.

We could even go ahead to establish a middle ground for sentiments that we do not consider as either negative or positve and refer to them as Neutral sentiment. A thorough understanding of what is being said about you by your customers allows you to see the areas of your business and strategues that require urgent attention/review and areas of your business that should be maintained and improved on. This process right here is made possible through sentiment analysis with machine learning.

How Do You Determine Your Probability of Successfully Accessing a Loan Facility?

Many factors influence a person's ability to access loans in varying amounts, mainly a person's observed ability to repay the loan which is proven by either an availability of certain assets the person can fall back on as guarantee, or a track record of sustainably collecting and repaying loans on time and in full (OTIF, an acronym that is cruicial in the supply chain industry, serving as a metric to guage each suppliers' performance if they supply goods on time and in full. You just learned something new 😊). In fact, there's a proven trend that there's a deterministic relationship between the ability to repay a loan and the probability of getting one.

This may sound intuitive but as researchers believe, nothing is proven until it is tested. One way to do one such test is to explore an actual data of loan recipients and some of their attributes to understand how these attributes affect their ability to secure a loan.
